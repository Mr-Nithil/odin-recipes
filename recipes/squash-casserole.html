Squash Casserole

Description

This creamy squash casserole is topped with buttery crackers and Cheddar cheese. Perfect for a tasty side or comforting vegetarian main dish. This creamy, cheesy, and buttery squash casserole is a Southern classic. This easy recipe simply combines fresh squash and onion with a creamy sauce and a crunchy cracker-Cheddar cheese topping.

Whether you serve this squash casserole as a flavorful side or a veggie-centric main dish, everyone will be begging you to make it again and again for family gatherings, potlucks, and holidays.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups sliced yellow squash
  • ½ cup chopped onion
  • ¼ cup water, or more as needed
  • 35 buttery round crackers, crushed
  • 1 cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • ¾ cup milk
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Steps

  1. Step 1
    2.

    Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Lightly grease a 9x13-inch casserole dish; set aside.

  2. Step 2
    3.

    Place squash, onion, and water in a large skillet over medium heat. Cover and cook until squash is tender, about 5 minutes. Drain well and transfer to a large bowl.

  3. Step 3
    4.

    Mix cracker crumbs and cheese together in a separate bowl. Stir half of the cracker mixture into cooked squash mixture.

  4. Step 4
    5.

    Mix eggs and milk together in a small bowl, then add to squash mixture; stir in 1/4 cup melted butter, and season with salt and pepper. Spread mixture into the prepared baking dish.

  5. Step 5
    6.

    Sprinkle with remaining cracker mixture, and dot with 2 tablespoons butter.

  6. Step 6
    7.

    Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown on top and set, about 25 minutes.

  7. Step 7
    8.

    Serve hot and enjoy!

Cook's Note

Stir in ½ cup sour cream or mayonnaise for tang and richness.

Serve alongside turkey or ham at Thanksgiving or Christmas. You can portion into two 8x8-inch pans—bake one now and freeze the other.
